Why Cold Storage Demand Is Rising in Pakistan

Published 2026-01-28 · By Izhar Foster Engineering

Pakistan's agriculture, food processing, and pharma sectors are growing fast — yet the cold-chain backbone supporting them has not kept pace. Post-harvest losses, broken vaccine cold chains, and limited frozen food capacity all point to a single market reality: cold storage demand is rising sharply, and businesses that build now will outpace those that wait.

The opportunity

For exporters, cold storage opens doors to high-value Western and Gulf markets that simply cannot be served by ambient logistics. For pharma, it enables compliance with international GDP standards. For dairy and meat, it stabilises supply against seasonal swings.

How to start

The right project starts with a clear statement of what you'll store, at what temperature, and for how long. From that, our engineers can size panels, refrigeration, doors, and controls — and price the project before you commit a rupee to civil works.
