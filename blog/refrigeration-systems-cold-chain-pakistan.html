Refrigeration Systems: Backbone of Pakistan's Cold Chain

Published 2026-01-28 · By Izhar Foster Engineering

Refrigeration systems are the literal engine of any cold-chain facility — and getting the engineering right under Pakistan's grid and climate conditions is harder than most international specifications suggest. 50°C ambients, voltage swings, intermittent power, and salt-laden coastal air all conspire against off-the-shelf solutions.

Ammonia vs Freon

For large industrial loads — meat processors, big distribution hubs, ice plants — ammonia (NH₃) systems remain the gold standard for thermodynamic efficiency. For medium-scale commercial and pharma applications, modern Freon systems offer simpler operation and lower regulatory burden.

Designing for Pakistan

Every plant we deliver is sized for high-ambient operation, with redundancy that survives planned and unplanned outages, condenser configurations that handle dust and salt, and control logic that recovers gracefully from grid disturbances.
