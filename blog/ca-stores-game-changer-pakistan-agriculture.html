CA Stores: A Game Changer for Pakistan's Agriculture Sector

Published 2026-01-28 · By Izhar Foster Engineering

Controlled Atmosphere (CA) storage is rapidly gaining ground in Pakistan, particularly among fruit growers and exporters who have long battled punishing post-harvest losses. By precisely regulating oxygen, carbon dioxide, nitrogen, humidity, and temperature inside a sealed cold room, CA technology slows the natural respiration of fresh produce — extending shelf life from weeks into many months.

Why it matters for Pakistan

Pakistan is one of the world's top fruit producers, but post-harvest losses for apples, dates, kinnow, and stone fruit routinely exceed 30%. CA storage is the single technology that can move the needle on those numbers — turning short, intense harvest gluts into year-round supply, and unlocking export windows that conventional cold storage simply cannot reach.

How a CA system actually works

A CA store starts with the same envelope as a high-performance cold room — PIR sandwich panels, a tightly sealed insulated door, and a refrigeration plant. To that we add gas monitoring sensors, nitrogen generation, CO₂ scrubbing, and an injection control system. Once the room is sealed and pulled down to target temperature, atmosphere control gradually displaces oxygen with nitrogen until the produce sits in a controlled mix — often 2–3% O₂ and 1–5% CO₂ depending on the crop.

What it means commercially

Apple growers in KPK can hold premium Royal Gala or Gala Must varieties out to April or May, hitting domestic price peaks instead of dumping at harvest. Date producers in Sindh extend marketability by months. Vegetable processors smooth supply for retail and food-service contracts.
