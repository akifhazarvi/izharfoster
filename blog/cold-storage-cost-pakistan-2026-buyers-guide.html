Cold Storage Cost in Pakistan — 2026 Buyer's Guide

Published 2026-04-30 · Updated 2026-04-30 · By Muhammad Anwar, GM Business Development

The most common question we get from prospective clients is "how much does cold storage cost in Pakistan?" — and the most honest answer is: it depends on six things, none of which are obvious from a Google search. This guide unpacks 2026 pricing in PKR for chiller, freezer, blast freezer, and CA store applications, including PIR sandwich panel prices, refrigeration system bands, and the hidden costs that separate a working facility from a money-pit. Numbers below are field-validated from Izhar Foster's 2,100+ delivered projects across Pakistan.

The six factors that decide your final number

Before any benchmark makes sense, lock down these six variables for your project:

  1. Operating temperature. A +4 °C chiller, a −20 °C freezer, and a −35 °C blast freezer have radically different equipment costs even at identical room volume.
  2. Volume in m³ (not m²). Cold storage capacity is volumetric. A 200 m² room with 4 m clear height (800 m³) costs more than the same footprint at 3 m height because every internal surface needs insulating.
  3. Panel thickness — directly tied to operating temperature and budgeted energy cost over 25 years. We'll break this down below.
  4. Refrigeration redundancy. A single-compressor design vs N+1 vs N+2 changes the equipment cost by 30–80%. For pharma and export applications it's not optional.
  5. Civil interface. Slab specification, drainage, vapour barrier, electrical service entry, generator integration. Often 10–18% of total project value, frequently under-budgeted.
  6. Validation and documentation. A vanilla commissioning vs IQ/OQ/PQ for pharma applications differs by PKR 0.5–3 million in engineering and documentation costs.

Why we don't quote PU on new cold-chain projects

Beyond the modest energy difference, PU has materially weaker fire performance (B2/B3 vs PIR's B1) and the WHO has flagged PU manufacturing chemistry concerns. Most modern Pakistani manufacturers (Izhar Foster included) discontinued PU for cold storage in favour of PIR. PU pricing remains in the table because legacy projects sometimes specify it — but for new builds, PIR is the engineering default.

Hidden costs that wreck cold-storage budgets

The line items below are missed in roughly four of every five budgets we review. Build them in at scoping rather than discovering them mid-project:

  • Voltage stabilisation: PKR 200,000–600,000 depending on plant capacity. Pakistani grid voltage swings will damage compressors without it.
  • Standby generator + ATS: PKR 1.5–4 million for 50–250 kVA capacity. Required for any export-grade or pharma application; essential commercial insurance even on domestic-market sites.
  • Civil slab + drainage + vapour barrier: typically PKR 4,500–8,500 per m² of footprint. The specification matters — undersized slab cracks, drainage failures cause refrigeration ice-up.
  • Electrical service upgrade: grid connection capacity, transformer sizing, switchgear. A 100 kW refrigeration load often needs a service upgrade the existing facility doesn't have.
  • Refrigeration commissioning + validation: baseline PKR 200,000–800,000 for a standard commissioning; PKR 1.5–4 million for IQ/OQ/PQ validation on pharma applications.
  • Service contract: typically 2–4% of equipment value annually. Skipping it is the single most common reason cold-stores have a ruinous compressor failure in years 6–8.
  • Spare-parts inventory: recommended PKR 300,000–1,500,000 of critical spares on-site. Pakistan import lead times for refrigeration components run 6–12 weeks.

ROI and payback by sector

The economics of a Pakistani cold storage depend heavily on how the asset is monetised:

Cold-storage-as-business (rental / 3PL)

Typical Pakistani rental yields run PKR 6–12 per kg per month for chilled storage and PKR 9–18 per kg per month for frozen. At 70–85% sustained occupancy, capital payback on a PKR 30 million 1,000 m³ freezer typically runs 4–6 years. Yields are highest in major commercial hubs (Karachi, Lahore, Islamabad) and during peak harvest windows (mango season for fruit, Eid for meat).

Captive use — food processor

For a dairy, meat processor, ice-cream manufacturer, or bakery using cold storage internally, ROI comes through reduced spoilage, better throughput planning, and longer market windows. Typical payback is 3–5 years for domestic-market operators and 18–36 months for export-oriented operators (where cold-chain integrity unlocks materially higher pricing).

Captive use — pharma manufacturer

Pharmaceutical cold storage is non-negotiable for any +2/+8 °C product (vaccines, biologics, insulins). The ROI calculation isn't payback per se — it's regulatory compliance and product-loss avoidance. A single excursion event can cost PKR 50–500 million in voided product. Properly-validated DRAP-compliant cold storage is risk insurance.

Cold storage as agricultural infrastructure

For commodity stores (potato, onion, mango, kinnow), economics depend on the spread between harvest-season prices and stored-product prices. A 1,000-tonne potato cold store in Punjab typically returns its capital in 3–4 seasons through arbitrage between glut and shortage windows. For premium fruit going to export markets, the multiplier is dramatically higher.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cost of cold storage in Pakistan in 2026?

A turnkey cold store ranges roughly PKR 18,000–28,000 per m³ for chiller, PKR 25,000–38,000 per m³ for freezer, and PKR 35,000–55,000 per m³ for blast freezer applications. For a typical 1,000 m³ freezer, this works out to PKR 28–35 million all-in (excluding civil works). Final price depends on capacity, panel thickness, refrigeration redundancy, and sector-specific validation requirements.

How much does a sandwich panel cost in Pakistan?

PIR panels in 2026 run roughly PKR 4,500–7,500 per m² for 50 mm, PKR 6,500–10,500 per m² for 100 mm, and PKR 9,500–15,500 per m² for 150 mm — factory-direct, steel-faced. PU panels are 10–15% cheaper than PIR; EPS panels 25–35% cheaper but with materially worse fire and thermal performance. Order volume, facer specification, and city of delivery all move the price.

Why are PIR panels more expensive than PU or EPS?

PIR has lower thermal conductivity (λ ≈ 0.020–0.022 W/m·K vs 0.024 for PU and 0.034 for EPS), better fire performance (class B1 vs B2/B3), and a 50+ year stable life. The 10–15% price premium typically pays back inside 4–6 years through reduced refrigeration energy on cold-chain applications. For ambient warehouse use, EPS or PU may be acceptable.

How much does it cost to build a 1,000 m³ cold storage in Pakistan?

A 1,000 m³ chiller at +2 to +4 °C with 100 mm PIR panels, two-compressor refrigeration with N+1 redundancy, electrical scope, controls, and validated commissioning typically lands around PKR 22–28 million in 2026. The same volume in standard freezer specification (−18 °C, 125 mm panels) lands around PKR 28–35 million. Add 15–20% for pharma-validated specification.

What is the ROI of investing in cold storage in Pakistan?

For commercial cold-storage-as-business, typical payback runs 4–6 years on rental yield (PKR 6–12 per kg per month chilled, PKR 9–18 frozen) at 70–85% occupancy. For captive food-processor use, payback comes through reduced spoilage and better throughput — typically 18–36 months for export-grade applications, 3–5 years for domestic-market.

How much electricity does a cold storage in Pakistan use?

A 1,000 m³ freezer at −20 °C uses 50,000–80,000 kWh per year — roughly PKR 2.0–3.5 million annually at industrial tariffs. A chiller at +4 °C uses 25,000–45,000 kWh per year. Energy cost over 25 years is the largest line item — often more than capital cost. Properly-specified panel thickness and modern controls cut this by 25–35%.

How does sandwich panel cost compare in Lahore vs Karachi vs Islamabad?

Sandwich panel prices vary modestly by city in Pakistan. Lahore is the baseline (factory is on Multan Road); Karachi adds ~3–5% for transport; Islamabad/Rawalpindi adds ~5–8%. Sialkot, Faisalabad, and Multan see the smallest premium. Bulk orders flatten the differential.

What are the hidden costs in a cold storage project?

Frequently-missed: voltage stabilisation, standby generator with ATS, civil slab specification (drainage, vapour barrier, electrical service upgrade), commissioning and validation documentation, ongoing service contract, and a critical-spares inventory. Together these can add 20–35% to a budget that ignored them. We list them all under Hidden costs above.

Should I choose ammonia or HFC refrigeration for industrial cold storage in Pakistan?

For large-tonnage industrial plants with trained operators, ammonia (R-717) is dramatically more energy-efficient and has zero GWP. For commercial and small-industrial applications, modern A2L blends like R-454C or R-449A are the right answer. We unpack the choice in detail in the green refrigeration guide.
