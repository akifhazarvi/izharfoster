Industries we engineer for

Each sector has its own band, its own auditor, its own failure mode.

Pharma cold rooms hold ±0.5 °C across a validated working volume — dairy chillers don't. Halal poultry chillers separate carcass-chill from cutting-room cold — fruit cold stores don't. Banana ripening cycles temperature on a stepped 4–6 day program — potato long-storage holds it constant for nine months. Choose the sector below to see how we engineer for it.

How we work

From sector brief to commissioning — a single engineering team.

Whichever industry you're in, the path is the same: brief, sized concept, validated design, install, commissioning with audit-ready documentation. What changes per sector is the temperature band, the tolerance, the regulatory frame, the redundancy, and the auditor's checklist. We've built that knowledge across seven decades, from a 277,460 sqft Lahore plant.

  1. Brief. Capacity, product, temperature band, regulatory frame, location, civil readiness.
  2. Sizing. Use our load calculator (ASHRAE Ch. 24) for the heat load, our cost calculator for the budget band.
  3. Concept design. Panel spec, refrigeration topology, controls philosophy, redundancy strategy.
  4. Validated design. Engineering drawings, refrigeration P&ID, electrical SLD, controls scope.
  5. Install & commissioning. Panel erection, refrigeration, controls, validation testing, sector-appropriate documentation.
  6. Handover. IQ/OQ/PQ for pharma, HACCP for food, training for operations.
