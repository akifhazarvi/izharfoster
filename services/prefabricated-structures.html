Conventional construction in Pakistan is slow, capital-intensive, and quality-variable. Prefabricated buildings move most of the work into a controlled factory environment — dramatically improving speed, repeatability, and lifecycle performance.

What we prefabricate

  • Cold storage warehouses and food factories
  • Industrial offices and admin blocks
  • School and college classrooms
  • Sports halls, gymnasiums, and stadium back-of-house
  • Labour housing, site accommodation, and remote-camp buildings
  • Shopping plazas and commercial mezzanine extensions
  • Modular guard rooms, utility cabins, and storage units

Why it works

Speed. A typical prefab warehouse is 2.5–3× faster to deliver than equivalent masonry construction.

Quality. Factory-controlled welding, fitting, and finishing produces fewer surprises and less site rework.

Thermal performance. PIR-clad envelopes deliver 30%+ energy savings versus brick or block.

Lifespan. 50+ years with proper maintenance.

Disassembly. Buildings can be relocated if your needs change — something masonry cannot offer.

Engineering scope

Steel structural frame, PIR sandwich wall and roof panels, insulated doors, windows, electrical first fix, plumbing connections, HVAC, and finishes — coordinated through one project manager.

Frequently asked

Prefabricated Structures — questions buyers ask.

The technical and commercial questions we hear most often before a contract is signed.

01How fast can you deliver?
A standard 1,000 m² warehouse is typically delivered and erected within 8–12 weeks of order, subject to foundation readiness.
02Can prefab buildings be relocated?
Yes. A clear advantage of bolted prefab structures is that they can be disassembled and reused at another site.
03Is the thermal performance good?
Significantly better than masonry, thanks to PIR cladding. Most prefab clients see 25–35% lower HVAC operating cost.
04Are they fire-safe?
Yes. PIR panels carry fire class B1 to ASTM E84, and steel framing follows applicable structural standards.
